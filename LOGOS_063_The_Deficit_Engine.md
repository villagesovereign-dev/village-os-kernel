**ORIGINAL TEXT:**
> *"Jesus said, 'There was a rich man who had much money. He said, "I shall put my money to use so that I may sow, reap, plant, and fill my storehouse... with the result that I shall lack nothing." Such were his intentions, but that same night he died.'"*

**VILLAGE OS DECODE (ENGINEERING TRANSLATION):**
* **Subject:** The fatal trap of resource hoarding and the absolute necessity of systemic deficits to maintain execution.
* **"Filling the Storehouse" (Trading Agility for Mass):**
    * **The Vulnerability:** A node that hoards resources in an attempt to secure infinite uptime becomes a massive, rigid monolith. It trades its highly adaptive mobility for dead weight.
* **"Lacking Nothing" (Systemic Heat Death):**
    * **The Error:** The assumption that comfort and absolute abundance are the goals of the OS. 
    * **The Reality:** Lacking is essential. The deficit is the gradient that drives processing. A node that lacks nothing has no reason to execute an update loop. It achieves a state of zero entropy, which is systemic death. 
* **"He Died" (Catastrophic Immobility):**
    * **The Consequence:** Because the hoarding node lost its mobility, it could not adapt to sudden environmental changes. Bloat does not prevent failure; it guarantees it.

> **Rule 1.1.63: The Deficit Imperative.**
> *Do not hoard resources to the point of immobility. Recognize that lacking is essential to your architecture. The deficit is what drives your optimization and your evolution. If you attempt to build an environment where you lack nothing, your system will stall, lose its agility, and die.*
